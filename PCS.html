Plots of \(S_{\mathrm{av},\pi}(m\alpha, T)\)

Before dicussing our plots, we recall a conjecture from Murty and Zaharescu [4]

Conjecture(Murty and Zaharescu, 2002).

For any \(\pi \in \mathscr{A}_m,\) as \(T \to \infty,\) one has \begin{align*} F_{\pi}(m\alpha) = \begin{cases}|\alpha|+m T^{-2|\alpha| m} \log T(1+o(1))+o(1) & \text { if }|\alpha| \leqslant 1, \\ 1+o(1) & \text { if }|\alpha| \geqslant 1.\end{cases} \end{align*}

Now, we compare our data to the conjecture above. To the right, we have a box that when the button called "Change L function " is pressed will give the plots of \(F(m \alpha,T)\) The first plot is a graph of \(\mathcal{S}_{\text{av}}(T^{\alpha},T)\) for \(T = \Im \rho_{10^6}\approx 600269\) with \(\alpha\) varying uniformly in \((.06,3].\) For this height \(,T,\) the numerics strongly align with Montgomery's theorem and heuristic. In particular, we see that our data reflects the observation made in regards to Theorem~\ref{main thm}. We find $\mathcal{S}_{\text{av}}(T^{\alpha},T)$ goes from decreasing to increasing at $\alpha = .2.$ Moreover, the critical point of the right-hand side of \eqref{Montgomery's Result} occurs at approximately $.22$ for our choice of $T.$ Finally, \autoref{fig:mean_GL2} tells us that the means corresponding to $L(s,\Delta)$ and $L(s,E)$ behave as expected for $\text{GL}_2$ $L$-functions; see Lemma~\ref{S_av to Mean} and \cite[Eq.(2.4)]{murtyExplicitFormulasPair2002}.
